# Evangelical Christian Content Recommendations for Regal Recovery App

## Research Date: April 8, 2026

## Executive Summary

This research identifies 45+ content recommendations across videos, podcasts, and articles from evangelical Christian ministries focused on sexual addiction recovery. The strongest content ecosystem comes from five core ministries: **Pure Desire Ministries** (best overall clinical + faith depth), **Covenant Eyes** (broadest content library), **Blazing Grace** (best grace-centered authenticity), **Conquer Series** (highest production quality video), and **Desiring God** (deepest theological rigor). A significant content gap exists for short-form daily devotional videos (under 10 minutes) specifically designed for men in sexual addiction recovery -- this represents an opportunity for Regal Recovery to produce original content. All recommendations have been vetted for evangelical Protestant theological alignment; one major ministry (XXXchurch/Craig Gross) has been flagged as theologically drifted and should be excluded.

**Important caveat:** All URLs and current availability are based on training data (knowledge cutoff) and were not live-verified due to network restrictions. Every URL must be manually verified before integration into the app.

- **Clinical camp** (Pure Desire, Doug Weiss, Mark Laaser): Integrates neuroscience and psychology with faith. Argues that understanding brain chemistry and trauma roots is essential for lasting recovery.
- **Bible-only camp** (Setting Captives Free, Proven Men): Emphasizes Scripture alone as sufficient. Argues that the "exchanged life" in Christ is the cure, not understanding your brain.
- **Resolution:** Include both. The clinical approach reaches men who need to understand the "why" behind their addiction. The Bible-only approach serves men who find strength in Scripture saturation. Let users gravitate toward what helps them. Present both as valid, complementary paths.

### Reformed vs. Broadly Evangelical
- **Reformed sources** (Desiring God, TGC, Harvest USA): Calvinist theological emphasis, sovereignty-focused
- **Broadly evangelical** (Celebrate Recovery, Covenant Eyes, Blazing Grace): Non-denominational, broadly accessible
- **Resolution:** Include both but ensure the app's own voice is broadly evangelical to be inclusive of all users. Reformed content tends to be more theologically rigorous; broadly evangelical content is more accessible.

### Behavioral vs. Heart Transformation
- **"Every Man's Battle" approach:** Focus on behavioral strategies ("bouncing your eyes," avoiding triggers)
- **Deeper approach** (Pure Desire, Surfing for God, Proven Men): Focus on root causes, heart idolatry, trauma, and desire
- **Resolution:** Behavioral strategies are useful as "first aid" during crisis moments. Heart transformation is the long-term goal. Present behavioral content as immediate tools and deeper content as the sustained healing path. Both are needed.
