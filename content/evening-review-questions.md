Great question. The 10th Step says "Continued to take personal inventory and when we were wrong promptly admitted it." The evening review is the daily practice of that step — an honest end-of-day accounting that prevents the slow drift back into denial, secrecy, and acting out.

Here are questions organized by the dimensions that matter most in sex addiction recovery, drawn from traditional 12-step literature, clinical recovery frameworks (Carnes, Dye, Laaser), and Christian discipleship practices:

**Sobriety & Integrity**
- Was I sober today in thought, word, and action?
- Did I entertain lustful thoughts or fantasies today, even briefly?
- Did I put myself in any risky situations — physically, digitally, or emotionally?
- Was there a moment today where I was close to acting out? What stopped me (or didn't)?
- Did I access anything I wouldn't want my spouse or sponsor to know about?
- Was I honest in every conversation today, or did I shade the truth, omit, or mislead?
- Did I keep a secret today that I should have shared?
- Did I delete anything from my phone, browser, or messages today?

**Emotional Health**
- What emotions did I experience today? Can I name at least three?
- Was there a feeling I tried to avoid, numb, or stuff down?
- Did I use anything as a mood-altering escape today — food, screens, shopping, work, sleep, fantasy?
- When did I feel most at peace today? When did I feel most agitated?
- Did I feel lonely today? What did I do with that loneliness?
- Did anger, resentment, or self-pity show up today? Toward whom?
- Am I carrying any shame right now? About what?

**Relational Health**
- Did I treat the people around me with respect and kindness today?
- Was I emotionally present with my spouse/family, or was I checked out?
- Did I act in (blame, criticize, stonewall, manipulate, control, avoid, hide, lie) toward anyone today?
- Did I owe anyone an amend today? Did I make it?
- Did I reach out to someone in my support network, or did I isolate?
- Is there a conversation I need to have that I'm avoiding?
- Did I put someone else's needs before my own in a healthy way today? Did I neglect my own needs in an unhealthy way?

**Spiritual Health**
- Did I spend time with God today — in prayer, scripture, or quiet listening?
- Did I sense God's presence at any point today? When?
- Was there a moment today where I relied on my own strength instead of surrendering to God?
- Am I holding anything back from God right now?
- What am I grateful for today? Can I name at least three things?
- Did I live with integrity today — the same person in private as in public?
- Is there something I need to confess — to God, to my sponsor, or to my spouse?

**Recovery Engagement**
- Did I work my recovery plan today? What did I complete? What did I skip and why?
- Did I attend a meeting, call my sponsor, or connect with an accountability partner?
- Did I use a recovery tool when I needed one, or did I white-knuckle through?
- Am I further along in my recovery today than I was yesterday? In what specific way?
- What is one thing I did today that the "old me" would not have done?

**FASTER Scale Awareness**
- Am I forgetting my priorities? Have I been skipping recovery practices?
- Am I feeling anxious or restless? About what?
- Am I speeding up — overcommitting, staying too busy, unable to rest?
- Am I ticked off — resentful, angry, blaming others?
- Am I exhausted — physically, emotionally, or spiritually depleted?
- Where am I on the FASTER Scale right now, honestly?

**Looking Forward**
- What is one thing I need to do differently tomorrow?
- Is there anything on tomorrow's calendar that could be a trigger or risk?
- What do I need from God tomorrow that I didn't ask for today?
- Who do I need to connect with tomorrow?
- What commitment am I making for the next 24 hours?

**For the app specifically**, I'd recommend the evening review be structured as a configurable set of questions — the user selects which questions to include from a library (like the morning commitment questions are already designed). A good default set for sex addiction recovery would be 5-7 questions that cover sobriety, honesty, emotions, relationships, and spiritual life without taking more than 5-10 minutes.

A strong default evening review set might be:

1. "Was I sober today in thought and action?" (Yes / Struggled but stayed sober / No)
2. "Was I fully honest today — no lies, omissions, or secrets?" (Yes / Mostly / No)
3. "What emotions did I experience today?" (Feelings Wheel selection)
4. "Did I act in toward anyone today?" (checklist from Acting In Behaviors)
5. "Did I connect with God and with another person in recovery today?" (Yes both / One of them / Neither)
6. "What am I grateful for today?" (free text)
7. "Where am I on the FASTER Scale right now?" (F / A / S / T / E / Restoration)
