# Reflective Journal Prompts

Recovery journal prompts for men in Sexaholics Anonymous and Celebrate Recovery. Organized by category with framework tags where applicable.

---

## 1. Daily Reflection

1. What am I most grateful for today, and what was the hardest part of my day?
2. Where did I see God at work in my life today, even in small ways?
3. What emotions did I experience today? Did I allow myself to feel them, or did I try to numb or avoid them?
4. Was I honest with everyone I interacted with today? If not, what held me back?
5. What recovery action did I take today (meeting, call, prayer, reading)?
6. Did I have any moments today where I felt disconnected from myself or others? What was happening?
7. How did I show up for someone else today?
8. What is one thing I would do differently about today if I could?
9. Rate your overall serenity today on a scale of 1-10. What contributed to that number?
10. What truth about yourself or your recovery became clearer today?
11. Did I pause before reacting to anything today? What happened when I did (or did not)?
12. What is one thing I need to surrender to God before I sleep tonight?
13. How well did I take care of my physical body today (sleep, food, exercise)? [FASTER]
14. Was there a moment today I felt truly present and alive? Describe it.
15. Write a brief honest letter to God about how your day went.

## 2. Sobriety & Urges

1. Describe the last time you experienced an urge. What was happening in your body, mind, and environment?
2. What is your sobriety telling you right now? Is it steady, fragile, or somewhere in between?
3. When you feel an urge rising, what is the very first physical sensation you notice? Where in your body do you feel it?
4. What does the "voice of your addiction" say to you when it wants you to act out? Write out its exact words. [3 Circles]
5. What healthy response have you used to ride out an urge recently? How did it feel afterward compared to how acting out would have felt?
6. Reflect on an urge you successfully resisted. What made the difference that time?
7. What middle-circle behaviors have you engaged in recently that could be moving you toward the inner circle? [3 Circles]
8. What outer-circle activities genuinely bring you life and joy? Are you doing enough of them? [3 Circles]
9. How has your understanding of sobriety deepened since you began recovery? What does sobriety mean to you beyond abstinence?
10. What "just this once" or "nobody will know" thoughts have surfaced recently? What truth can you replace them with?
11. Describe a time your sobriety felt like a gift rather than a burden. What shifted your perspective?
12. Are there any situations you are currently putting yourself in that test your sobriety? Be honest about what you are risking and why.
13. What does "progressive victory over lust" look like for you in practical terms this week?
14. Write about the cost of relapse. Not to shame yourself, but to remember what is at stake.
15. "No temptation has overtaken you except what is common to mankind. And God is faithful; he will not let you be tempted beyond what you can bear" (1 Cor 10:13). How does this truth land with you today?

## 3. Emotional Awareness

1. What emotion are you avoiding right now? What would happen if you let yourself feel it fully? [FASTER]
2. Where are you on the FASTER Scale today? Be specific about the signs you are noticing. [FASTER]
3. Are you Forgetting Priorities right now? What recovery commitments have you been letting slide? [FASTER]
4. What is making you anxious this week? How are you responding to that anxiety -- with healthy coping or with escape? [FASTER]
5. Are you Speeding Up? List everything on your plate right now. Are you using busyness to avoid something? [FASTER]
6. Who or what are you Ticked Off about? Have you expressed that anger directly and honestly? [FASTER]
7. How exhausted are you -- physically, emotionally, spiritually? What is draining you, and what needs to change? [FASTER]
8. Describe a time in the past week when you noticed yourself going emotionally numb. What triggered the shutdown?
9. What emotion did you most often confuse with another growing up? (For example, was sadness expressed as anger in your home?)
10. When you feel lonely, what do you typically do? Is that response moving you toward connection or isolation?
11. Write about a time you felt deep joy in recovery. What made it possible?
12. What does anger usually protect you from feeling? Look underneath it and describe what is really there.
13. How do you experience shame in your body? What does it feel like, and what thoughts come with it?
14. Name five emotions you felt today. For each one, write one sentence about what caused it.
15. Reflect on the difference between feeling your feelings and being controlled by them. Where are you with that right now?

## 4. Relationships & Trust

1. What is the current state of trust between you and your spouse or closest relationship? Be specific about what is rebuilding and what remains broken.
2. What did you share in your last FANOS or FITNAP check-in that was hard to say? What did it feel like afterward? [FANOS/FITNAP]
3. What are you currently withholding from your spouse or accountability partner? What is the fear underneath the withholding?
4. How has your addiction affected your ability to be emotionally intimate (not sexually) with your partner?
5. Describe what rebuilding trust looks like from your side. What specific actions are you taking consistently?
6. What needs are you trying to get met through your addiction that could be met in healthy relationships? [FANOS/FITNAP]
7. How do you respond when your spouse expresses pain about your past behavior? Do you get defensive, shut down, or stay present?
8. Write about one way you practiced honesty in a relationship this week, even when it was uncomfortable.
9. How is your relationship with your accountability partner or sponsor? Are you holding anything back from them?
10. What does your spouse or partner need from you right now that you are struggling to give? What is getting in the way?
11. Reflect on the difference between being honest and being fully transparent. Which one does your recovery require right now?
12. How has betrayal trauma affected your spouse? What have you learned about their pain that you did not understand before?
13. What affirmation can you genuinely offer your spouse or accountability partner today? [FANOS/FITNAP]
14. Write about a relationship in your life where you feel safe to be fully known. What makes that relationship work?
15. "Therefore, confess your sins to each other and pray for each other so that you may be healed" (James 5:16). How are you practicing this in your recovery relationships?

## 5. Spiritual Growth

1. Describe your current relationship with God in one honest paragraph. No filter, no performance.
2. What does "turning your will and life over to the care of God" actually look like in your daily life? [12-Step]
3. When do you feel closest to God? When do you feel most distant? What patterns do you notice?
4. What are you praying for right now? Are there things you are afraid to bring to God?
5. How has your understanding of God changed since you entered recovery?
6. Write about a time you experienced God's grace in the middle of your brokenness.
7. What spiritual practices are most life-giving for you right now (prayer, scripture, worship, silence, nature, service)?
8. Reflect on Step 2: "Came to believe that a Power greater than ourselves could restore us to sanity." What does "sanity" look like for you? [12-Step]
9. How do you distinguish between God's voice and the voice of your shame? What does each one sound like?
10. What scripture has meant the most to you in your recovery journey? Why?
11. "He brought me out into a spacious place; he rescued me because he delighted in me" (Psalm 18:19). What does it mean to you that God delights in you, even knowing everything?
12. Write a prayer about your recovery in your own honest words. Do not make it sound good -- make it sound real.
13. Where are you trying to white-knuckle your recovery instead of relying on God's power?
14. Reflect on Step 11: "Sought through prayer and meditation to improve our conscious contact with God." How is your conscious contact right now? [12-Step]
15. What does surrender mean to you today? Is there something you are still holding onto?

## 6. Shame & Identity

1. What is the difference between guilt ("I did something bad") and shame ("I am bad")? Which voice is louder in your life right now?
2. When shame speaks to you, what does it say? Write its words out and then write what you know to be true next to each one.
3. What messages about yourself did you absorb in childhood that still drive your behavior today?
4. How has your addiction shaped your identity? How are you beginning to see yourself apart from it?
5. Reflect on this: "Therefore, there is now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus" (Romans 8:1). Do you live as though you believe this?
6. What are you most afraid people would think of you if they knew your full story?
7. Describe a moment in recovery when you felt genuinely worthy of love. What made that moment possible?
8. How does shame show up in your body? What physical sensations accompany it? What do you typically do to escape those sensations?
9. What is one true, good thing about you that your addiction has tried to erase? Claim it in writing.
10. Who in your life speaks truth over your identity? Who reinforces shame? How can you increase time with the former?
11. Write about the man you are becoming in recovery. Not the man you were, not a perfect future version -- the real man who is doing this hard work right now.
12. How do you practice self-compassion without using it as an excuse to avoid accountability?
13. What would change in your life if you truly believed you were loved unconditionally by God?
14. Reflect on the difference between humility and self-hatred. Where do you tend to land?
15. "See what great love the Father has lavished on us, that we should be called children of God! And that is what we are!" (1 John 3:1). Sit with this verse. What comes up?

## 7. Triggers & Patterns

1. What are your top three triggers right now? Have they changed since you started recovery? [3 Circles]
2. Describe the sequence of events, thoughts, and emotions that typically precede a slip or relapse. Map it out step by step.
3. What time of day are you most vulnerable? What is it about that time that makes it harder?
4. What environmental triggers do you need to address but have been avoiding? (Devices, locations, routines.)
5. Describe your typical "acting-in" behaviors. How do they show up before you ever reach acting out? [3 Circles]
6. How does the HALT framework (Hungry, Angry, Lonely, Tired) apply to your patterns? Which one is your most common entry point?
7. What role does fantasy play in your addiction cycle? How early does it start, and how do you interrupt it?
8. What relational triggers are hardest for you to manage (conflict, rejection, criticism, feeling unseen)?
9. Are there any patterns connected to specific seasons, anniversaries, or life events? Describe them.
10. What cognitive distortions ("I deserve this," "Just once," "It does not count") appear most frequently in your thinking?
11. Review your PCI indicators. Which areas of life manageability are slipping right now? [PCI]
12. Map out your middle-circle behaviors. Are any of them creeping closer to the inner circle? [3 Circles]
13. Describe a time you recognized a trigger early and successfully intervened. What did you do?
14. What does your "ritual" look like -- the predictable sequence between trigger and acting out? Name each step honestly. [3 Circles]
15. What healthy replacement behaviors have you established for your most common triggers? Are they working, or do they need adjusting?

## 8. Amends & Accountability

1. Reflect on Step 1: Where in your life are you still trying to maintain the illusion of control over your addiction? [12-Step]
2. What does a thorough and fearless moral inventory look like for you? What are you most reluctant to include? [12-Step]
3. Who have you harmed through your addiction? List the people and briefly note how each was affected. [12-Step]
4. What amends do you need to make that you have been putting off? What is holding you back? [12-Step]
5. Describe a living amend you are currently making. What does it look like in daily practice? [12-Step]
6. How honest are you with your accountability partner or sponsor on a scale of 1-10? What would it take to move one number higher?
7. What character defects (Step 6) are you becoming ready to have God remove? Which ones are you still clinging to? [12-Step]
8. Reflect on Step 10: "Continued to take personal inventory and when we were wrong promptly admitted it." How promptly are you admitting wrongs? [12-Step]
9. What is the hardest truth you have had to tell someone in recovery? What happened when you told it?
10. How do you distinguish between making amends and seeking forgiveness? What is the difference in posture?
11. Write about a time your honesty in accountability led to unexpected freedom or connection.
12. Are there amends that would cause more harm than good? How do you navigate that tension wisely? [12-Step]
13. What does Step 3 -- turning your will over to God -- look like in a practical decision you are facing right now? [12-Step]
14. How has doing step work changed your understanding of yourself?
15. What does it mean to carry the message to other addicts (Step 12) in a way that is authentic to where you are? [12-Step]

## 9. Gratitude & Hope

1. List three things about your recovery you are grateful for today, no matter how small.
2. What is one way your life is genuinely better now than it was when you were actively in your addiction?
3. Who is someone in recovery who inspires you? What about their journey gives you hope?
4. Describe a moment of unexpected beauty or goodness in your life this week.
5. What relationship has improved since you began recovery? How has it changed?
6. What can you do today that you could not do a year ago because of the growth you have experienced?
7. Write a letter of encouragement to yourself six months ago, when recovery was newer or harder.
8. What is one promise from Scripture that anchors your hope today?
9. "He who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion" (Philippians 1:6). How do you see God's ongoing work in your recovery?
10. What milestone in your recovery are you most proud of? Sit with that accomplishment without minimizing it.
11. Name one character quality that has grown in you through recovery (patience, honesty, empathy, humility).
12. What would you tell a man on day one of recovery? What do you wish someone had told you?
13. How has suffering in recovery produced something good in your life that you would not trade?
14. Reflect on a time God met you in your lowest moment. What did that encounter teach you?
15. Write about the future you are working toward. Not a fantasy -- a grounded, honest vision of the life recovery is making possible.

## 10. Deep Work

_These prompts are designed for structured reflection, ideally with the support of a sponsor, therapist, or CSAT. Take your time. Be honest. Be kind to yourself._

1. What is your earliest memory of using sexual behavior to manage an emotion or escape a situation? How old were you, and what were you feeling? [Arousal Template]
2. Describe the emotional and relational environment of your childhood home. Where did you learn to hide, perform, or shut down?
3. What unmet needs from childhood are you still trying to meet through your addiction? (Connection, validation, comfort, control, escape.) [Arousal Template]
4. How did your family of origin handle conflict, emotions, and intimacy? What patterns did you absorb?
5. What attachment style do you tend toward (anxious, avoidant, disorganized)? How does it show up in your relationships and your addiction cycle?
6. Map your arousal template: What emotional states, situations, and types of fantasy have historically fueled your acting out? Look for the pattern beneath the pattern. [Arousal Template]
7. What was your first exposure to pornography or sexual content? How did that experience shape your understanding of sex, intimacy, and yourself?
8. What losses or traumas have you never fully grieved? How might unprocessed grief be connected to your addiction?
9. Write about the "double life" you maintained during your active addiction. What was the cost of living divided? What did it take to hold that together?
10. What is the deepest wound your addiction is trying to medicate? If the addiction is the anesthetic, what is the pain?
11. How has your relationship with your father (or father figure) shaped your sexuality, your view of masculinity, and your sense of worth?
12. Describe the emotional state you are most afraid to feel. What would happen if you let yourself fully experience it without escaping?
13. What lies about yourself did you believe before recovery that you are only now beginning to question? Where did those lies come from?
14. Reflect on any cycle of abuse, addiction, or dysfunction in your family history. How has that cycle shown up in your life, and what are you doing to break it?
15. Write honestly about what intimacy means to you -- not sex, but true emotional and spiritual closeness with another person. What makes it terrifying? What makes it worth pursuing?

## Prompt Metadata

**Total prompts:** 150 (15 per category)

**Framework references:**
- `[FASTER]` -- FASTER Scale (Forgetting Priorities, Anxiety, Speeding Up, Ticked Off, Exhausted, Relapse)
- `[3 Circles]` -- Inner/Middle/Outer Circle behavioral boundaries
- `[12-Step]` -- SA 12 Steps and related step work
- `[FANOS/FITNAP]` -- Structured check-in frameworks for couples and accountability
- `[PCI]` -- Personal Craziness Index life manageability indicators
- `[Arousal Template]` -- Patrick Carnes arousal template framework

**Design notes:**
- Each category contains a mix of quick-response prompts and deeper reflection prompts
- Scripture references are included naturally, not forced onto every prompt
- All prompts are phrased as questions or invitations
- Language is compassionate and direct, never shaming
- Prompts assume SA sobriety definition (no sex with self, no sex with anyone other than spouse)
