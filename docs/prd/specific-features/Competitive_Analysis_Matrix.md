## 1. Complete Competitor Profiles

### Fortify

- **Developer:** Impact Collective LLC (acquired by Malouf; affiliated with Fight the New Drug nonprofit)
- **Platform:** iOS, Android, Web (app.joinfortify.com)
- **Pricing:** $12.00/month, $89.99/year, $199.99 lifetime (prices increased from earlier $9.95/month)
- **Faith Integration:** None — explicitly secular and science-based (cultural ties to LDS community but no religious content in-app)
- **Target Audience:** Anyone struggling with pornography; both genders; all ages (13+)
- **Core Features:** 15+ tailored learning Journeys, PaxAI® 24/7 AI recovery coach (Premium), streak tracking, journaling, community forums, daily check-ins, personalized insights
- **Traction:** 500,000+ app downloads across 150+ countries (up from 200K+ previously reported)
- **Ratings:** iOS 4.7★ (3,200+ ratings); Android ~3.75★ (~3,600 reviews, 339,000+ installs)
- **Strengths:** Most established recovery-focused platform; strong educational content; PaxAI coach is industry-leading AI feature; non-shaming secular approach broadens appeal
- **Weaknesses:** No content blocking/filtering; no spouse/partner tools; no faith integration; Android ratings significantly lower than iOS; free tier reduced after PaxAI launch

### Covenant Eyes (now Victory Shield)

- **Developer:** Covenant Eyes, Inc. (private, employee-owned ESOP since 2020, Owosso, MI)
- **Platform:** iOS, Android, Windows, macOS (Chromebook NOT supported)
- **Pricing:** $18/month, $184/year, $850 lifetime (app store prices slightly higher: $18.99/month, $199.99/year). All plans cover unlimited devices and up to 10 users.
- **Faith Integration:** Strong Christian (core identity — named after Job 31:1; broadly ecumenical)
- **Target Audience:** Christian families, individuals seeking accountability; marketed heavily through churches
- **Core Features:** Screen Accountability™ AI (captures screenshots, analyzes for explicit content, sends blurred reports to "allies"), content blocking, Victory companion app (courses including Strive® for men and Arise™ for women, community, partner dashboard)
- **Traction:** 1.7M+ lifetime users (company claim); estimated ~130K active paying subscribers; estimated $23-27M ARR (third-party algorithmic estimates from Growjo, RocketReach, Datanyze — not independently confirmed; CE is private and discloses no financials)
- **Ratings:** iOS ~4.3★; Google Play 4.5-4.6★ (57,000+ reviews); Trustpilot 3.7/5 (54 reviews — polarized: 31% five-star, 48% one-star)
- **Strengths:** 25-year incumbent; massive Christian market penetration; comprehensive device coverage; strong brand recognition; AI screenshot analysis is unique technology
- **Weaknesses:** Monitoring-only — no recovery tools; iOS monitoring limited to Safari and CE browser only; privacy concerns about screenshots of personal documents; pricing complaints; no Chromebook support
- **Note:** Major 2025 rebrand from "Covenant Eyes" to "Victory Shield" across all platforms

### Ever Accountable

- **Developer:** Ever Accountable LLC
- **Platform:** Android, iOS, Windows, macOS, Chromebook, Kindle, Chrome extension (broadest platform coverage)
- **Pricing:** Individual $9.99/month or $88.99/year; Family $12.99/month or $118.99/year; Ascend tier $147.99; Chrome extension $9.99/month (updated October 2025)
- **Faith Integration:** Not explicitly branded as Christian but marketed heavily to Christian audiences; testimonials reference faith
- **Target Audience:** Individuals and families seeking accountability; strong church referral channel
- **Core Features:** On-device AI monitoring, instant alerts to accountability partners, weekly reports, multi-device coverage
- **Traction:** Undisclosed user base
- **Ratings:** iOS 4.2★ (~1,000 ratings); Android 4.5★ (5,200+ reviews). Last updated March 25-27, 2026.
- **Strengths:** Broadest platform coverage (7 platforms); lower price than Covenant Eyes; instant alerts
- **Weaknesses:** No recovery tools; no community; monitoring-only; less brand recognition than Covenant Eyes

### Pure Desire Ministries

- **Developer:** Pure Desire Ministries International
- **Platform:** Web application (app.puredesire.org) — no standalone mobile app in current stores
- **Pricing:** Pricing page currently returns 404; workbooks $15-25 each
- **Faith Integration:** Strong Christian (core identity) — CSAT/PSAP certified clinicians with pastoral experience
- **Target Audience:** Men, women, and couples recovering from sexual addiction and betrayal trauma; churches
- **Core Features:** 8-10 month online support groups, individual and couples counseling via video, workbook curricula (Seven Pillars of Freedom, Betrayal & Beyond), two podcasts (440+ episodes), church leader training, SAST screening, free eBooks
- **Traction:** 100,000+ people served over 30+ years; co-published "Beyond the Porn Phenomenon" with Barna Group (2024); ~8 clinical staff currently
- **Ratings:** N/A (no app store presence)
- **Strengths:** Deep clinical credibility; 30+ years trust; strong church partnerships; comprehensive couples/spouse programming; Barna research partnership
- **Weaknesses:** No mobile app; no daily digital tools; high counseling cost; "93% effectiveness" claim unverified (no disclosed methodology)

### Brainbuddy

- **Developer:** Brainbuddy Pty Ltd
- **Platform:** iOS, Android
- **Pricing:** $12.99/month (hard paywall); additional tiers $37.99-$99.99; individual courses $7.99; 7-day free trial
- **Faith Integration:** None (secular, neuroscience-based)
- **Target Audience:** Primarily male; individuals seeking to quit pornography
- **Core Features:** 100-day neuroscience program, content blocker, gamification, daily lessons, habit tracking
- **Traction:** ~760,000+ Android downloads (Google Play shows 500K+ tier)
- **Ratings:** iOS 4.7★ (26,000 ratings); Android 4.6★ (~26,000 reviews) — among highest in competitive set
- **Strengths:** Strong neuroscience content; excellent ratings; integrated content blocker; well-designed gamification
- **Weaknesses:** Hard paywall; 100-day program implies endpoint; no faith; no spouse tools

### rTribe / Four Streams

- **Developer:** Recovery Tribe, Inc. — acquired; now under ForgeWorks Business Development
- **Platform:** Android only (iOS app removed from App Store)
- **Pricing:** Free to download; $5.99/month or $59.99/year
- **Faith Integration:** None — pivoted fully to secular personal/professional growth
- **Target Audience:** Corporate wellness and personal growth (no longer recovery-focused)
- **Traction:** ~100,000+ Google Play downloads (historical)
- **Ratings:** iOS removed; Android ~4,600 reviews (historical)
- **Competitive relevance:** Minimal — effectively exited the recovery market

### Relay

- **Developer:** Relay Health (YC Summer 2022 batch)
- **Platform:** iOS, Android (correct URL: joinrelay.app)
- **Pricing:** $11.99 and $22.99/month; $99, $149, and $299/year; scholarships available; 30-day money-back guarantee
- **Faith Integration:** Optional — ~50% of users choose "faith + psychology" track; founder is LDS; science always foundational
- **Target Audience:** Anyone struggling with pornography/compulsive sexual behavior; gender-separated groups
- **Core Features:** Group-based peer accountability (3-10 anonymous peers), therapist-designed 16-week program, weekly Zoom meetings, AI insights
- **Traction:** 122,000+ total downloads (company claim; Google Play shows only 10,000+)
- **Ratings:** 4.8★ on both platforms (iOS: 1,600 ratings; Android: ~1,970 reviews) — highest-rated alongside NOMO
- **Strengths:** Best peer group model; Y Combinator backing; excellent ratings; optional faith track
- **Weaknesses:** Group quality varies; high premium pricing; no individual tools; no content filtering; no spouse tools

### NOMO

- **Developer:** Parker Stech (single independent developer, in recovery)
- **Platform:** iOS, Android, iPad, Mac (M1+) — NOT Android-only as previously reported
- **Pricing:** Completely free, no in-app purchases. Companion: Nomo Widgets ($0.99)
- **Faith Integration:** None
- **Target Audience:** Anyone tracking sobriety from any addiction
- **Core Features:** Multiple sobriety clocks, accountability partners, milestone sharing, Connect Wall community
- **Traction:** ~96,000-100,000 Google Play downloads; iOS is the stronger platform
- **Ratings:** iOS 4.8★ (15,000 ratings); Android ~4.1★ (~1,400 ratings). Updated March 29, 2026.
- **Strengths:** Completely free; authentic; actively maintained; multi-addiction; simple
- **Weaknesses:** Very basic (tracker only); no content, education, or guided programs; single developer risk

### I Am Sober

- **Developer:** Hungry Wasp Studios
- **Platform:** iOS, Android (no web app)
- **Pricing:** Free; Plus: $9.99/month or $39.99/year (reduced from $119.88/year); 7-day trial
- **Faith Integration:** None
- **Target Audience:** General — any addiction
- **Core Features:** Sobriety tracker, daily pledges, milestones, check-ins, community (500K+ members), savings calculator
- **Traction:** 11M+ downloads; ~200,000 downloads/month (early 2026)
- **Ratings:** Google Play 4.76★ (~120,000-130,000 reviews); iOS ~4.8★ — highest review volume in competitive set
- **Strengths:** Massive scale; excellent ratings; simple UX; strong community
- **Weaknesses:** Entirely generic; no clinical tools; no content filtering; no faith; no spouse tools

### Celebrate Recovery App

- **Developer:** Celebrate Recovery
- **Platform:** iOS (15+), Android
- **Pricing:** Completely free
- **Faith Integration:** Strong Christian (core identity)
- **Target Audience:** Anyone with hurts, habits, hang-ups — not sex-addiction-specific
- **Core Features:** Group finder, step-study tracking, Tools (8 Principles, 12 Steps, Serenity Prayer), podcast (161+ episodes), devotionals, events, Mighty Networks community
- **Traction:** 35,000+ churches (current website; some sources cite 37,000+); 5M+ Step Study participants (possibly 7M+ total reached)
- **Ratings:** iOS 4.6★ (52 reviews); Android limited reviews
- **Strengths:** Massive church infrastructure; free; strong brand; broad appeal
- **Weaknesses:** Minimal app (group finder, not recovery tool); no tracking/journaling/check-ins; outdated meeting data; bugs reported

### The Freedom Fight

- **Developer:** Student Mobilization / Freedom Fight Organization
- **Platform:** iOS, Android, Web (app.thefreedomfight.org)
- **Pricing:** Free ($0 — 30-Day Challenge, FASTER Scale, community); FF Program ($19.99/month × 7 months or $99 one-time); Premium Groups ($98-$119/month)
- **Faith Integration:** Strong Christian (core — "Christ-centered discipleship," Statement of Faith)
- **Target Audience:** Christian men and women seeking freedom from pornography/sex addiction
- **Core Features:** 29-week program, 60+ video lessons, 60+ Bible studies, FASTER Scale (free), accountability messaging, 30-Day Challenge, small groups with certified leaders
- **Traction:** 10,000+ Google Play downloads; 172 Android reviews
- **Ratings:** Google Play 4.12★ (172 reviews); iOS insufficient ratings — **previously claimed 4.9★ is not substantiated**
- **Strengths:** Closest to Regal Recovery's positioning; FASTER Scale free; comprehensive gospel curriculum; certified leader model
- **Weaknesses:** Actual ratings far below claimed 4.9; crash/bug reports; data loss issues; "86% success rate" is self-reported excluding dropouts

### QUITTR

- **Developer:** QUITTR Inc. (co-founders Alex Slater, Connor McLaren)
- **Platform:** iOS, Android
- **Pricing:** Hard paywall; ~$12.99/month or ~$20-45/year; ~$50 "limited" lifetime (vs. stated $450 "regular")
- **Faith Integration:** Partial/minimal
- **Target Audience:** Primarily male; pornography
- **Core Features:** Panic Button (front camera), "Melus" AI chatbot, streak tracking, diagnostic quiz
- **Traction:** 1.5M claimed (likely total downloads, not active); reportedly ~$500K/month revenue
- **Ratings:** iOS ~4.7-4.8★ (~24K-29K reviews); Google Play 4.3★ (~9,400 reviews); JustUseApp safety 33.4/100
- **⚠️ CRITICAL:** Major 2025-2026 data breach exposed 600,000+ users (including ~100,000 minors) via misconfigured Firebase database. Exposed: ages, masturbation frequency, emotional responses, porn habits. Additionally: misleading diagnostic quiz, aggressive billing, inability to restore purchases, app renamed 4+ times
- **Competitive relevance:** Cautionary tale — strongest argument for Regal Recovery's privacy-first architecture

## 3. Regal Recovery's Unique Value Proposition vs. Each Competitor

### vs. Fortify
**Fortify is the strongest recovery-focused competitor, but it's explicitly secular and recently raised prices 21%.** Regal Recovery's UVP: "Everything Fortify offers in recovery tools — plus faith integration, content filtering, spouse/couples tools, 12-step work, clinical assessments (FASTER, PCI, SAST-R), and a therapist portal — at a lower monthly price ($9.99 vs. $12.00). For the 54% of practicing Christians who struggle with pornography and say secular tools don't speak to their recovery."

### vs. Covenant Eyes / Victory Shield
**Covenant Eyes is the 25-year incumbent in accountability with estimated $23-27M ARR, but it's a monitoring tool — not a recovery platform.** Regal Recovery's UVP: "Covenant Eyes tells you what happened on your screen. Regal Recovery helps you understand why it happened, what to do about it, and how to heal — with content filtering built into the same platform. One app replacing two subscriptions at a lower price ($14.99 vs. $18/month) with dramatically more recovery functionality. And unlike Covenant Eyes, full cross-browser monitoring on iOS — not just Safari."

### vs. Ever Accountable
**Ever Accountable has the broadest platform coverage (7 platforms) and recently diversified pricing.** Regal Recovery's UVP: "Ever Accountable monitors your device. Regal Recovery monitors your device AND provides the complete recovery toolkit that turns accountability data into actual behavior change. Comparable price with 20x the value."

### vs. Pure Desire Ministries
**Pure Desire has the deepest clinical credibility in Christian sex addiction recovery with 30+ years and 100K+ served, but no mobile app.** Regal Recovery's UVP: "Pure Desire's clinical expertise meets daily digital recovery tools. Regal Recovery is the mobile companion Pure Desire's programs need but don't have. Positioned as a complement and partnership opportunity, not competition."

### vs. Brainbuddy
**Brainbuddy has excellent ratings (4.7/4.6★ with 52K+ combined reviews) and strong neuroscience content, but hard paywall and no faith.** Regal Recovery's UVP: "Brainbuddy locks everything behind a paywall before trust is built. Regal Recovery's generous free tier lets you experience value first. Brainbuddy's 100-day program implies an endpoint; Regal Recovery is a lifelong companion integrating brain science with faith, community, and spouse healing."

### vs. Relay
**Relay has the best peer group model, highest ratings (4.8★), and Y Combinator backing.** Regal Recovery's UVP: "Relay's group accountability is powerful but narrow. Regal Recovery provides the comprehensive individual toolkit that supports the other 167 hours between group sessions. Relay is the meeting. Regal Recovery is the rest of the week."

### vs. NOMO
**NOMO is free, authentic, and actively maintained by a single developer in recovery.** Regal Recovery's UVP: "NOMO tells you how many days you've been sober. Regal Recovery tells you why you relapsed, what your triggers are, how your emotional health is trending, and what to do about it. The difference between counting days and building a life."

### vs. I Am Sober
**I Am Sober has massive scale (11M+ downloads) proving demand, but is entirely generic.** Regal Recovery's UVP: "I Am Sober works for any addiction but specializes in none. Regal Recovery is built for sex addiction from the ground up — with purpose-built tools that I Am Sober cannot and will not offer."

### vs. Celebrate Recovery App
**The CR App is a companion to the world's largest church recovery program (35K+ churches), but it's a minimal group finder.** Regal Recovery's UVP: "The CR App helps you find a meeting. Regal Recovery is what you use the other 6 days and 22 hours between meetings. Positioned as the recommended digital companion for CR participants."

### vs. The Freedom Fight
**The closest competitor to Regal Recovery's positioning — deeply Christian with free FASTER Scale. But actual ratings (4.12★) and technical quality fall far short of claims (4.9★).** Regal Recovery's UVP: "Same heart, dramatically more capability and technical quality. Plus comprehensive tracking, PCI, clinical assessments, content filtering, spouse/couples mode, AI chatbot, meeting finder, and a platform that doesn't crash and lose data."

### vs. QUITTR
**QUITTR's 2025-2026 data breach exposing 600K+ users makes it an anti-pattern.** Regal Recovery's UVP: "In a space where trust is everything, QUITTR proved what happens when privacy is an afterthought. Regal Recovery's privacy-first architecture — on-device processing, field-level encryption, HIPAA-adjacent handling — is the antidote."
