## 1. Executive Summary/Purpose

### 1.1 Why Are We Building This?

**The Problem:**
Sexual addiction, often co-occurring with multiple other addictive behaviors, affects millions of individuals and their families. Current recovery solutions are fragmented—requiring users to juggle multiple apps, journals, meeting schedules, and support networks. Those seeking recovery from a Christian perspective face an additional challenge: most secular recovery apps lack spiritual integration, while faith-based resources often lack the sophisticated digital tools necessary for daily accountability and progress tracking.

**The Opportunity:**
We are building Regal Recovery to provide a comprehensive, integrated recovery platform that addresses the whole person—mentally, intellectually, physically, emotionally, and spiritually—from a Christian worldview. This app will serve as a "digital recovery companion" that combines evidence-based addiction recovery methodologies with Christian discipleship, community accountability, and intelligent tracking.

**Business Objectives:**
- Establish market leadership in faith-based addiction recovery technology
- Achieve 50,000 active users
- Create sustainable revenue through freemium model with premium content partnerships
- Build a supportive community that reduces isolation and increases recovery success rates
- Develop partnerships with Christian counseling organizations and recovery ministries

**Success Definition:**
Users will experience increased sobriety duration, reduced relapse frequency, improved mental health metrics, and strengthened spiritual practices, supported by measurable engagement with daily tools and community features.

## 2. Target Audience

### 2.1 Primary Audience

**Core User Profile:**
- **Demographics:** Males and females, ages 18-65, predominantly 25-45
- **Geographic:** English and Spanish-speaking regions (US, Canada, UK, Australia, Mexico, and Latin America initially)
- **Faith Background:** Christians (all denominations) seeking faith-integrated recovery
- **Addiction Profile:** Primary sex addiction with common co-addictions (pornography, substance use, gambling, shopping, eating disorders)
- **Recovery Stage:** All stages—from pre-contemplation to long-term maintenance
- **Tech Proficiency:** Moderate to high; comfortable with smartphone apps and digital tools
- **Economic Status:** Middle income; willing to pay for premium resources that support recovery

### 2.2 Secondary Audiences

1. **Spouses/Partners:** Individuals supporting someone in recovery, needing their own tools and education
2. **Sponsors/Accountability Partners:** Mentors providing guidance and oversight
3. **Therapists/Counselors:** Licensed professionals managing client recovery through the platform
4. **Recovery Coaches:** Certified coaches coordinating recovery activities
5. **Ministry Leaders:** Church and para-church organization staff facilitating group recovery programs

### 2.3 User Segmentation

- **Segment A - Early Recovery (0-90 days):** High risk, needs intensive daily tools and accountability
- **Segment B - Active Recovery (90 days - 2 years):** Building habits, needs structure and milestone tracking
- **Segment C - Maintenance (2+ years):** Sustained sobriety, needs community and ongoing growth tools
- **Segment D - Multi-Addiction:** Complex recovery needs requiring multiple tracking systems
- **Segment E - Couples in Recovery:** Both individual and relational healing needs

## 3. User Personas & Pain Points

### 3.1 Primary Persona: "Addict Alex"

**Demographics:**
- Age: 34
- Occupation: IT Project Manager
- Location: Suburban Dallas, TX
- Marital Status: Married to Sarah, 2 children (ages 4 and 7)
- Faith: Evangelical Christian, attends church regularly
- Recovery Stage: Early recovery (45 days sober)

**Background:**
Alex has struggled with pornography and sex addiction for 15 years. After his wife Sarah discovered his behavior, he committed to recovery. He attends a weekly 12-step meeting, sees a Christian therapist bi-weekly, and has a sponsor (Sam) in his recovery group. He's motivated but overwhelmed by managing recovery alongside work and family demands.

**Goals:**
- Maintain daily sobriety and rebuild trust with his wife
- Develop healthy coping mechanisms for stress and triggers
- Integrate his faith meaningfully into recovery
- Track progress and celebrate milestones
- Connect with others who understand his struggle

**Pain Points:**
- **Fragmented Tools:** Uses paper journals, separate tracking apps, and scattered resources
- **Trigger Vulnerability:** Experiences sudden urges with no immediate intervention tool
- **Shame & Isolation:** Feels alone in his struggle, fears judgment from church community
- **Accountability Gaps:** Struggles to maintain daily consistency without external accountability
- **Spiritual Disconnect:** Finds it hard to connect scripture and prayer to practical recovery
- **Time Constraints:** Limited time for recovery work between job and family responsibilities
- **Relapse Fear:** Anxious about slip-ups and doesn't have a structured relapse prevention plan
- **Partner Relationship:** Doesn't know how to involve his wife appropriately in his recovery

**Technology Behaviors:**
- Uses iPhone 13, Apple Watch
- Active on limited social media (deleted Instagram due to triggers)
- Prefers apps with clean, simple interfaces
- Uses voice-to-text frequently while commuting
- Values privacy and security features

**Quote:** *"I want to get better, but I feel like I'm juggling ten different things and nothing fits together. I need something that helps me stay focused every single day and reminds me why I'm doing this."*

### 3.3 Tertiary Persona: "Coach Caleb"

**Demographics:**
- Age: 45
- Occupation: Certified Recovery Coach
- Location: Fort Worth, TX
- Marital Status: Married, 2 teenage children
- Faith: Non-denominational Christian, active in recovery ministry
- Recovery Stage: 8 years sober, certified recovery coach

**Background:**
Caleb has been in recovery for 8 years and became a certified recovery coach 3 years ago. He works with both men and women in recovery, managing a caseload of 6-8 clients at any time. He partners with therapists and sponsors to provide holistic support but struggles to coordinate across all the people involved in each client's recovery.

**Goals:**
- Effectively coach multiple clients simultaneously with clear progress visibility
- Coordinate with each client's broader support team (therapist, sponsor, spouse)
- Provide timely encouragement and accountability between sessions
- Track clients' progress and identify warning signs early
- Share resources and assignments efficiently

**Pain Points:**
- **Communication Overload:** Receives texts/calls at all hours from different clients
- **Progress Tracking:** Hard to maintain a clear picture of each person's recovery trajectory
- **Coordination Gap:** No single place to see what the therapist assigned vs. what the sponsor recommended
- **Resource Sharing:** Manually sends articles, scripture, and tools repeatedly
- **Availability Limits:** Can't always respond immediately during crisis moments
- **Engagement Uncertainty:** Doesn't know if clients are doing their recovery work between sessions

**Technology Behaviors:**
- Uses Android Samsung Galaxy
- Moderate to high tech adoption, uses scheduling and note-taking apps
- Likes dashboard views that show key information quickly
- Values tools that reduce administrative overhead

**Quote:** *"I can see the progress my clients are making, but I wish I had a single place to track everything and coordinate with their sponsors and therapists without endless group texts."*
